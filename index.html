Carl Sagan's timeline

  • 1934 - Born in Brooklyn, New York.
  • 1948 - Moved with his family to Rahway, New Jersey.
  • 1951 - Graduated from Rahway High School.
  • 1955 - Received a B.S. degree in physics from the University of Chicago.
  • 1957 - Married biologist Lynn Margulis.
  • 1960 - Earnt a Ph.D. degree from the same university.
  • 1961 - Started working as an assistant professor in Harvard University.
  • 1961 - His contributions were central to the discovery of the high surface temperatures of the planet Venus.
  • 1968 - After being denied a tenure at Harvard, accepted an offer to become a faculty member at Cornell University.
  • 1968 - Married artist Linda Salzman.
  • 1970 - Became a full professor at Cornell and directed the Laboratory for Planetary Studies there.
  • 1972 - Assembled the first physical message that was sent into space, a plaque attached to space probe Pioneer 10.
  • 1977 - Helped to develop and assemble the Voyager Golden Record message that was sent to space with the Voyager spaces probes.
  • 1980 - Co-wrote and narrated the award-winning 13-part television series Cosmos: A Personal Voyage. The book, Cosmos, written by Sagan, was published to accompany the series.
  • 1981 - Married author Ann Druyan.
  • 1994 - Received the Public Welfare Medal, the highest award of the National Academy of Sciences for "distinguished contributions in the application of science to the public welfare".
  • 1996 - After suffering from myelodysplasia for two years, and receiving three bone marrow transplants from his sister Carol, Sagan died of pneumonia at the age of 62, at the Fred Hutchinson Cancer Research Center in Seattle, Washington, in the early morning of December 20, 1996. Burial took place at Lakeview Cemetery in Ithaca, New York.
